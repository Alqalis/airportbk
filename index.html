Sir Ahmadu Bello International Airport

(SABIA) is an airport serving Birnin Kebbi in the Kebbi State of Nigeria. It is located at Ambursa, 16 kilometres (9.9 mi) east of Birnin Kebbi, along the Birnin Kebbi-Argungu road. The runway was extended from its original 2,200 metres (7,200 ft) to 3,100 metres (10,200 ft) and had a new ramp and terminal built in 2013. The Birnin Kebbi DVOR-DME (Ident: BIK) is located on the field.

Also Instrument Landing System (ILS) Localiser (ident IRK ) and Glideslope is installed at runway 06. The airport started full operations in 2014, and since then Muslim pilgrims from Kebbi state were flown from SABIA to Jedda International Airport in Saudi Arabia. The airport also has scheduled and unscheduled domestic flights. The scheduled flights to Abuja were via Airpeace prior to MAX Air, NG Eagle Air, which is currently the major airline operating from SABIA.

About

Birnin Kebbi International Airport (Sir Ahmadu Bello International Airport) serves Birnin Kebbi, the capital city of Kebbi State, northwestern Nigeria. The airport was inaugurated by the Nigerian President in Nov-2014. The airport features a 3200m x 60m runway capable of handling Boeing 747-400 aircraft primarily used for pilgrimage services. Birnin Kebbi is managed by the Federal Airports Authority of Nigeria.
